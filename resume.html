Resume Support

Craft a Resume that Tells Your Story

Your resume is often your first impression-an opportunity to highlight your unique strengths, skills, and experiences to potential employers. Whether you are starting from scratch or polishing an existing resume, these resources are here to help at every stage.

UMSI students are in high demand, and a clear, well-organized resume helps you stand out. On this page, you will find practical tips, templates, and guides tailored to School of Information students and aligned with employer expectations.

Need feedback? Meet with a CDO Career Coach to review your materials.

Resume Writing Steps

  • Download a UMSI-Approved Template: Start with a format that works-no need to design from scratch.
  • Gather Your Experiences: List jobs, internships, volunteer roles, coursework, and projects relevant to your goals.
  • Write Impactful Bullets: Use action verbs and show your skills, contributions, and results.
  • Tailor for Each Opportunity: Customize your resume for each job or internship.
  • Review and Edit: Proofread, check formatting, and ask for feedback from peers or a coach.

Resume FAQ

  • How long should my resume be?

    For most UMSI students and new grads, one page is best. More experienced professionals may require two pages.

  • Do I need a different resume for each application?

    It is wise to tailor your resume to the requirements and keywords of each opportunity.

  • Is it okay to include coursework or student projects?

    Absolutely. UMSI coursework and projects are valued by employers, especially when they show relevant skills and impact.

  • How often should I update my resume?

    Update your resume every semester, after major projects, or whenever you gain a new role or responsibility.
