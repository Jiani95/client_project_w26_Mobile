Welcome to the Career Development Networking and Connections Hub

Intro to Make Connections

Building connections with alumni is one of the most effective ways for students to explore career paths and secure jobs or internships. Networking consistently ranks as the top way opportunities are found, and alumni are often the most valuable contacts because they share a common educational background and are more inclined to help fellow students. Alumni can offer first-hand insight into specific industries and companies, provide honest perspectives on roles and career trajectories, and share practical advice on how to position yourself as a UMSI student to become more competitive and marketable. In fact, the number one career recommendation from the Career Development Office is to network with alumni, and that process often begins with a thoughtful email outreach.

Steps for You to Build Your Connections

  • First: Find Alumni UMSI CDO offers several ways to find alumni connections.

    These links can help you get started:

    • UMSI Linkedin group

      This is a Linkedin group many UMSI alumni have joined. You can search inside the group to find alumni and start conversations.

    • UMSI Career link

      Log in with your UMSI CareerLink SSO to access career development resources for UMSI students and alumni.

    • UCAN

      University Career Alumni Network platform for building connections between students and professionals.

  • Second: Get a Response Send messages to alumni, and do not get discouraged by lower response rates. Average response rate to cold outreach is around 20%. Each connection is still a valuable addition to your network.

    Schedule a coffee chat, phone call, or email conversation with alumni to build rapport through thoughtful outreach.

    Review these DOs and DON'Ts before sending your first message:

    • DOs: Keep your message concise, time-bound, and personal. Respect their time with a clear ask (for example, a 20-minute chat within two weeks).

    • DON'Ts: Do not ask for information already available online. Do not send your resume without permission. Do not start by asking for a referral or job.
